About Me

Hi, I'm Joe. I've been gazing at maps and playing around with computers for as long as I can remember and I've been doing it for a living since 2010. I believe that maps and the web can help us make sense of the huge volume of data out there and begin to understand the world around us.

I'm a consultant at Forte Consulting in Victoria, BC. I previously worked as a GIS Developer at WSP Group, a multidisciplinary engineering company in the UK.

Recently I worked on the design, development and data management of the Victoria Recycling Maps project, with Bruce Mackenzie.

I love the outdoors and spend my time exploring Vancouver Island by foot, mountain bike and kayak. When I'm home I like to map the places I've spent time getting to know by editing OpenStreetMap. Occasionally I map places in need of humanitarian relief that I haven't visited before.

I'm a firm believer in open data and open source software. I try to contribute and utilize it when I can. I stopped using Windows at home in 2010 in favour of Ubuntu and I've since made the leap over to Linux at work too .

My knowledge spans from web development, scripting and object oriented programming, to spatial/relational databases and more "traditional" GIS, like map making and spatial analysis.

I have a Master of Science (MSc) degree, with distinction, in GIS and a Bachelor of Science (BSc) degree in Geology and Physical Geography from the University of Edinburgh.
